Constants

$k=\dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}=8.99\times10^{9}\ \mathrm{N\cdot m^2/C^2}$  ·  $\varepsilon_0=8.85\times10^{-12}\ \mathrm{C^2/(N\cdot m^2)}$  ·  $e=1.6\times10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$  ·  $\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\ \mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$  ·  $1\ \mathrm{eV}=1.6\times10^{-19}\ \mathrm{J}$

Unit 1 — Electrostatics

$F=k\dfrac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}$Coulomb's law (magnitude)
$\vec E=k\dfrac{q}{r^2}\hat r,\qquad \vec F=q\vec E$Electric field of a point charge; force on a charge
$\Phi_E=\oint \vec E\cdot d\vec A=\dfrac{q_{\text{in}}}{\varepsilon_0}$Gauss's law
$V=k\dfrac{q}{r},\qquad U=k\dfrac{q_1q_2}{r}$Potential and potential energy of point charges
$E=\dfrac{V}{d}\ \text{(uniform)},\qquad \Delta U=q\,\Delta V$Field between plates; energy change
$C=\dfrac{Q}{\Delta V},\qquad U=\tfrac12 C(\Delta V)^2=\dfrac{Q^2}{2C}$Capacitance and stored energy
$C_\text{parallel}=\textstyle\sum C_i,\qquad \dfrac{1}{C_\text{series}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac{1}{C_i}$Capacitor combinations

Unit 2 — Circuits

$I=\dfrac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t},\qquad V=IR$Current; Ohm's law
$R=\rho\dfrac{L}{A}$Resistance from resistivity
$P=IV=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$Electrical power
$R_\text{series}=\textstyle\sum R_i,\qquad \dfrac{1}{R_\text{parallel}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac{1}{R_i}$Resistor combinations
$\tau=RC,\qquad q(t)=Q\!\left(1-e^{-t/RC}\right)$RC charging

Unit 3 — Magnetism

$\vec F=q\vec v\times\vec B,\qquad F=qvB\sin\theta$Magnetic force on a moving charge
$r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}$Radius of circular motion in a field
$\vec F=I\vec L\times\vec B,\qquad \tau=NIAB\sin\theta$Force on a wire; torque on a loop
$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}\ \text{(wire)},\quad B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}\ \text{(loop centre)}$Fields from currents
$\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc},\qquad B=\mu_0 n I\ \text{(solenoid)}$Ampère's law; solenoid

Unit 4 — Induction

$\Phi_B=\int \vec B\cdot d\vec A=BA\cos\theta$Magnetic flux
$\varepsilon=-N\dfrac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$Faraday's law (Lenz's law = the minus sign)
$\varepsilon=BLv$Motional EMF
$\varepsilon=-L\dfrac{dI}{dt},\qquad U=\tfrac12 LI^2$Inductance; energy stored
$\tau=\dfrac{L}{R},\qquad \omega=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}$RL time constant; LC angular frequency

Exam-day strategy

A 6-step routine for every problem

  1. Read twice & list givens with units. Note what's asked, in what form (e.g. "in unit-vector notation", "in eV").
  2. Draw a diagram. Mark charges/currents, directions, and a coordinate axis. Most lost points come from sign and direction mistakes a sketch prevents.
  3. Pick the formula that links givens to the unknown. Write it symbolically first.
  4. Solve algebraically, then substitute numbers with units last. Keep $k$, $\varepsilon_0$, $\mu_0$ as needed.
  5. Check the answer: right units? sensible magnitude? correct sign/direction?
  6. Box it and state the units. Partial credit lives in shown steps — always show your work.

Top mistakes that cost points

  • Forgetting that the field inside a conductor is zero in electrostatics.
  • Mixing up series vs parallel — capacitors add in parallel, resistors add in series.
  • Sign of work/energy: a positive charge speeds up moving from high to low potential.
  • Using diameter instead of radius, or mm instead of m. Convert everything to SI first.
  • Dropping the direction in $\vec v\times\vec B$ — use the right-hand rule, and flip for negative charges.

Read each topic's Concept section once for understanding, memorize the Formulas, then close the page and try to reproduce the Worked Examples on paper before reading the steps.
