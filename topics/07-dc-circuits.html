Unit 2 · Circuits — Topic 7

DC Circuits

Real circuits combine sources and resistors. Kirchhoff's two rules solve any network; RC circuits add the time dimension.

Concept

A battery supplies an EMF $\varepsilon$ — energy per coulomb. Real batteries have a small internal resistance $r$, so the terminal voltage is $V=\varepsilon-Ir$.

Resistors combine with rules opposite to capacitors:

Combining resistors

  • Series: same current; resistances add: $R_\text{eq}=\sum R_i$.
  • Parallel: same voltage; reciprocals add: $\dfrac1{R_\text{eq}}=\sum\dfrac1{R_i}$.

Kirchhoff's laws handle anything more complex:

  • Junction (current) rule: charge is conserved, so current in = current out at every node: $\sum I_\text{in}=\sum I_\text{out}$.
  • Loop (voltage) rule: energy is conserved, so the voltage changes around any closed loop sum to zero: $\sum \Delta V=0$.

An RC circuit charges or discharges a capacitor through a resistor with a characteristic time $\tau=RC$. After one $\tau$ the capacitor reaches about 63% of its final charge; after $5\tau$ it's essentially done.

Key Formulas

$\varepsilon=I(R+r),\qquad V_\text{term}=\varepsilon-Ir$EMF with internal resistance
$R_\text{series}=\textstyle\sum R_i,\qquad \dfrac1{R_\text{parallel}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac1{R_i}$Resistor combinations
$\sum I_\text{in}=\sum I_\text{out},\qquad \sum_\text{loop}\Delta V=0$Kirchhoff's rules
$\tau=RC,\qquad q(t)=Q\!\left(1-e^{-t/\tau}\right)$RC charging

ε = EMF (V)  ·  r = internal resistance (Ω)  ·  τ = time constant (s).

Worked example Series–parallel resistor network

A $12\,\mathrm V$ battery connects to a $4\,\Omega$ resistor in series with a parallel pair of $6\,\Omega$ and $3\,\Omega$. Find the total current from the battery and the voltage across the parallel section.

Step 1 — parallel pair

$\dfrac1{R_p}=\dfrac16+\dfrac13=\dfrac12\Rightarrow R_p=2\,\Omega$.

Step 2 — total resistance

$R_\text{eq}=4+2=6\,\Omega$.

Step 3 — total current

$I=\dfrac{V}{R_\text{eq}}=\dfrac{12}{6}=2.0\,\mathrm A$.

Step 4 — voltage across the parallel part

$V_p=I\,R_p=(2.0)(2)=4.0\,\mathrm V$ (the $4\,\Omega$ drops the other $8\,\mathrm V$).

Answer$I=2.0\,\mathrm A$, $V_p=4.0\,\mathrm V$

Practice

Practice 1

Resistors of $4.0\,\Omega$, $6.0\,\Omega$, and $2.0\,\Omega$ are in series across a $12\,\mathrm V$ battery. Find the equivalent resistance and the current.

$R_\text{eq}=4+6+2=12\,\Omega$.

$I=\dfrac{V}{R_\text{eq}}=\dfrac{12}{12}$.

Answer$R_\text{eq}=12\,\Omega$, $I=1.0\,\mathrm A$.

Why: series ⇒ add resistances, same current everywhere.

Practice 2

Find the equivalent resistance of $6.0\,\Omega$ and $12.0\,\Omega$ in parallel.

$R_\text{eq}=\dfrac{R_1R_2}{R_1+R_2}=\dfrac{6\times12}{18}$.

Answer$R_\text{eq}=4.0\,\Omega$.

Why: parallel ⇒ product/sum (two resistors); result below the smaller.

Practice 3

A $24\,\mathrm V$ battery feeds an $8.0\,\Omega$ resistor in series with two $4.0\,\Omega$ resistors that are in parallel. Find the total current.

Parallel pair: $\dfrac{4\times4}{8}=2.0\,\Omega$.

$R_\text{eq}=8.0+2.0=10\,\Omega$, so $I=\dfrac{24}{10}$.

Answer$I=2.4\,\mathrm A$.

Why: reduce the parallel block first, then it is one series loop.

Practice 4

A battery has emf $12\,\mathrm V$ and internal resistance $0.50\,\Omega$. When it delivers $4.0\,\mathrm A$, find the terminal voltage.

$V=\varepsilon-Ir=12-(4.0)(0.50)$.

Answer$V_\text{term}=10\,\mathrm V$.

Why: internal resistance drops $Ir$ from the emf.

Practice 5

Two batteries, $9\,\mathrm V$ and $3\,\mathrm V$, are connected in series aiding (same direction) with $2.0\,\Omega$ and $4.0\,\Omega$ in series. Find the current.

Aiding emfs add: $\varepsilon=9+3=12\,\mathrm V$. $R=2+4=6\,\Omega$.

$I=\dfrac{12}{6}$.

Answer$I=2.0\,\mathrm A$.

Why: aiding emfs add (opposing would subtract).

Practice 6

A single loop has a $10\,\mathrm V$ battery with $2.0\,\Omega$ and $3.0\,\Omega$ in series. Find the voltage across the $3.0\,\Omega$ resistor.

$I=\dfrac{10}{2+3}=2.0\,\mathrm A$.

$V_{3\Omega}=IR=(2.0)(3.0)$.

Answer$V_{3\Omega}=6.0\,\mathrm V$.

Why: find loop current, then $V=IR$ on that resistor.

Practice 7

A circuit dissipates power in a single $6.0\,\Omega$ resistor across $12\,\mathrm V$. Find the power.

$P=\dfrac{V^2}{R}=\dfrac{(12)^2}{6.0}$.

Answer$P=24\,\mathrm W$.

Why: $P=V^2/R$ when you know the voltage across $R$.

Practice 8

In an RC circuit, $R=2.0\times10^3\,\Omega$ and $C=5.0\,\mu\mathrm F$. Find the time constant.

$\tau=RC=(2.0\times10^3)(5.0\times10^{-6})$.

Answer$\tau=0.010\,\mathrm s$ (10 ms).

Why: $\tau=RC$.

Practice 9

A $2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor charges through a resistor from a $10\,\mathrm V$ source. How much charge does it hold after one time constant?

Full charge: $Q_\text{max}=CV=(2.0\times10^{-6})(10)=20\,\mu\mathrm C$.

After one $\tau$: $q=0.63\,Q_\text{max}=0.63(20)$.

Answer$q\approx12.6\,\mu\mathrm C$.

Why: one $\tau$ ⇒ 63% of the final charge.

Practice 10

At steady state in a DC circuit, a $12\,\mathrm V$ battery drives $2.0\,\Omega$ in series, then a capacitor sits in parallel with a $4.0\,\Omega$ resistor. Find the current through the capacitor and the voltage across it.

At steady state the capacitor is fully charged, so no current flows into it.

All current goes through the $2$–$4\,\Omega$ path: $I=\dfrac{12}{6}=2.0\,\mathrm A$, and $V_C=V_{4\Omega}=IR=2.0(4.0)=8.0\,\mathrm V$.

Answer$I_\text{cap}=0$; $V_C=8.0\,\mathrm V$.

Why: steady-state capacitor = open branch; its voltage matches what it parallels.
