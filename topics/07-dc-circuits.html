DC Circuits

Real circuits combine sources and resistors. Kirchhoff's two rules solve any network; RC circuits add the time dimension.

Concept

A battery supplies an EMF $\varepsilon$ — energy per coulomb. Real batteries have a small internal resistance $r$, so the terminal voltage is $V=\varepsilon-Ir$.

Resistors combine with rules opposite to capacitors:

Combining resistors

  • Series: same current; resistances add: $R_\text{eq}=\sum R_i$.
  • Parallel: same voltage; reciprocals add: $\dfrac1{R_\text{eq}}=\sum\dfrac1{R_i}$.

Kirchhoff's laws handle anything more complex:

  • Junction (current) rule: charge is conserved, so current in = current out at every node: $\sum I_\text{in}=\sum I_\text{out}$.
  • Loop (voltage) rule: energy is conserved, so the voltage changes around any closed loop sum to zero: $\sum \Delta V=0$.

An RC circuit charges or discharges a capacitor through a resistor with a characteristic time $\tau=RC$. After one $\tau$ the capacitor reaches about 63% of its final charge; after $5\tau$ it's essentially done.

Key Formulas

$\varepsilon=I(R+r),\qquad V_\text{term}=\varepsilon-Ir$EMF with internal resistance
$R_\text{series}=\textstyle\sum R_i,\qquad \dfrac1{R_\text{parallel}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac1{R_i}$Resistor combinations
$\sum I_\text{in}=\sum I_\text{out},\qquad \sum_\text{loop}\Delta V=0$Kirchhoff's rules
$\tau=RC,\qquad q(t)=Q\!\left(1-e^{-t/\tau}\right)$RC charging

ε = EMF (V)  ·  r = internal resistance (Ω)  ·  τ = time constant (s).

Worked example Series–parallel resistor network

A $12\,\mathrm V$ battery connects to a $4\,\Omega$ resistor in series with a parallel pair of $6\,\Omega$ and $3\,\Omega$. Find the total current from the battery and the voltage across the parallel section.

Step 1 — parallel pair

$\dfrac1{R_p}=\dfrac16+\dfrac13=\dfrac12\Rightarrow R_p=2\,\Omega$.

Step 2 — total resistance

$R_\text{eq}=4+2=6\,\Omega$.

Step 3 — total current

$I=\dfrac{V}{R_\text{eq}}=\dfrac{12}{6}=2.0\,\mathrm A$.

Step 4 — voltage across the parallel part

$V_p=I\,R_p=(2.0)(2)=4.0\,\mathrm V$ (the $4\,\Omega$ drops the other $8\,\mathrm V$).

Answer$I=2.0\,\mathrm A$, $V_p=4.0\,\mathrm V$

Practice

Practice 1

Three $9\,\Omega$ resistors are connected in parallel across a $6\,\mathrm V$ battery. Find the equivalent resistance and the total current.

$\dfrac1{R_\text{eq}}=\dfrac19+\dfrac19+\dfrac19=\dfrac39=\dfrac13\Rightarrow R_\text{eq}=3\,\Omega$.

$I=\dfrac{V}{R_\text{eq}}=\dfrac{6}{3}=2.0\,\mathrm A$.

Answer$R_\text{eq}=3\,\Omega$, $I=2.0\,\mathrm A$

Practice 2

A $2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor charges through a $1.0\,\mathrm{M}\Omega$ resistor. What is the time constant, and roughly how long until the capacitor is essentially fully charged?

$\tau=RC=(1.0\times10^{6})(2.0\times10^{-6})=2.0\,\mathrm s$.

"Fully" charged $\approx 5\tau=10\,\mathrm s$.

Answer$\tau=2.0\,\mathrm s$; fully charged after $\approx10\,\mathrm s$
