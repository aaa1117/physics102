Sources of Magnetic Field

Where do magnetic fields come from? Moving charges and currents. Ampère's law is the magnetic twin of Gauss's law — symmetry makes it easy.

Concept

Electric currents create magnetic fields. The Biot–Savart law is the fundamental recipe, but for the exam you mostly need its standard results:

  • A long straight wire: field circles the wire, $B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$, with direction set by the right-hand rule (thumb along $I$, fingers curl along $\vec B$).
  • At the centre of a circular loop of radius $R$: $B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$.
  • Inside a long solenoid with $n$ turns per metre: $B=\mu_0 n I$, uniform and along the axis.

Ampère's law

For currents with enough symmetry, $\displaystyle\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc}$ gives $B$ in one step — choose an "Amperian loop" along which $B$ is constant. It's the magnetic analogue of Gauss's law.

The constant $\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$ is the permeability of free space. Two parallel wires carrying current attract (same direction) or repel (opposite) — the basis of the definition of the ampere.

Key Formulas

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$Long straight wire (distance $r$)
$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$Centre of a circular loop
$B=\mu_0 n I$Inside a long solenoid ($n$ = turns/m)
$\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc}$Ampère's law

$\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$  ·  n = turns per metre  ·  r, R in metres.

Worked example Field of a straight wire and a solenoid

(i) Find $B$ at $2.0\,\mathrm{cm}$ from a long wire carrying $10\,\mathrm A$. (ii) Find $B$ inside a solenoid with $500$ turns over $0.25\,\mathrm m$ carrying $3.0\,\mathrm A$.

(i) Straight wire

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}=\dfrac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(10)}{2\pi(0.020)}=1.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm T$.

(ii) Solenoid

$n=\dfrac{500}{0.25}=2000\,\mathrm{turns/m}$, so $B=\mu_0 n I=(4\pi\times10^{-7})(2000)(3.0)=7.5\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$.

Answer$B_\text{wire}=1.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm T$; $B_\text{solenoid}\approx7.5\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$

Practice

Practice 1

Find the magnetic field $0.10\,\mathrm m$ from a long straight wire carrying $5.0\,\mathrm A$.

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}=(2\times10^{-7})\dfrac{5.0}{0.10}$.

Answer$B=1.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: $\mu_0/2\pi=2\times10^{-7}$; field $\propto1/r$.

Practice 2

A long vertical wire carries current upward. In which direction does its magnetic field point at a location due east of the wire?

Right-hand rule: thumb up along $I$, fingers curl around the wire.

East of the wire, the field points into the page (i.e. toward the north–south line, here 'into the ground' / away from you).

AnswerInto the page (curling around the wire).

Why: grip the wire with the right hand, thumb along $I$; fingers give $\vec B$.

Practice 3

Find the field at the centre of a single circular loop of radius $0.050\,\mathrm m$ carrying $3.0\,\mathrm A$.

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}=\dfrac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(3.0)}{2(0.050)}$.

Answer$B\approx3.8\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: loop centre: $B=\mu_0 I/2R$.

Practice 4

A flat coil of $50$ turns and radius $0.10\,\mathrm m$ carries $2.0\,\mathrm A$. Find the field at its centre.

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 NI}{2R}=\dfrac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(50)(2.0)}{2(0.10)}$.

Answer$B\approx6.3\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: $N$ turns multiply the single-loop field.

Practice 5

A solenoid has $500$ turns per metre and carries $2.0\,\mathrm A$. Find the field inside.

$B=\mu_0 nI=(4\pi\times10^{-7})(500)(2.0)$.

Answer$B\approx1.3\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: solenoid: $B=\mu_0 nI$, uniform inside.

Practice 6

Two long parallel wires $0.10\,\mathrm m$ apart each carry $10\,\mathrm A$ in the same direction. Find the force per length, and state whether they attract or repel.

$\dfrac{F}{L}=\dfrac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d}=(2\times10^{-7})\dfrac{(10)(10)}{0.10}$.

Same direction ⇒ attraction.

Answer$F/L=2.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{N/m}$, attractive.

Why: parallel currents attract; $F/L=\mu_0I_1I_2/2\pi d$.

Practice 7

An Ampèrian loop encircles a long wire carrying $8.0\,\mathrm A$. Evaluate $\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell$.

Ampère's law: $\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc}=(4\pi\times10^{-7})(8.0)$.

Answer$\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell\approx1.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{T\!\cdot\! m}$.

Why: the line integral depends only on enclosed current.

Practice 8

In the Biot–Savart law, for what angle between $d\vec\ell$ and $\hat r$ is the contribution $dB$ largest?

$dB\propto\sin\theta$, which is largest at $\theta=90^\circ$.

Answer$90^\circ$ (perpendicular).

Why: the $\sin\theta$ factor peaks at a right angle; it is zero when parallel.

Practice 9

Two long parallel wires $0.20\,\mathrm m$ apart carry $10\,\mathrm A$ in opposite directions. Find the field at the midpoint.

Each wire is $0.10\,\mathrm m$ from the midpoint: $B=(2\times10^{-7})\dfrac{10}{0.10}=2.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$.

Opposite currents ⇒ the two fields point the same way at the midpoint and add.

Answer$B=4.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: opposite currents reinforce between the wires (same currents would cancel).

Practice 10

A toroid has $N=1000$ turns and carries $I=2.0\,\mathrm A$; the field circle has radius $r=0.10\,\mathrm m$. Find $B$ inside.

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 NI}{2\pi r}=(2\times10^{-7})\dfrac{(1000)(2.0)}{0.10}$.

Answer$B\approx4.0\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$.

Why: toroid: $B=\mu_0 NI/2\pi r$.
