Sources of Magnetic Field

Where do magnetic fields come from? Moving charges and currents. Ampère's law is the magnetic twin of Gauss's law — symmetry makes it easy.

Concept

Electric currents create magnetic fields. The Biot–Savart law is the fundamental recipe, but for the exam you mostly need its standard results:

  • A long straight wire: field circles the wire, $B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$, with direction set by the right-hand rule (thumb along $I$, fingers curl along $\vec B$).
  • At the centre of a circular loop of radius $R$: $B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$.
  • Inside a long solenoid with $n$ turns per metre: $B=\mu_0 n I$, uniform and along the axis.

Ampère's law

For currents with enough symmetry, $\displaystyle\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc}$ gives $B$ in one step — choose an "Amperian loop" along which $B$ is constant. It's the magnetic analogue of Gauss's law.

The constant $\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$ is the permeability of free space. Two parallel wires carrying current attract (same direction) or repel (opposite) — the basis of the definition of the ampere.

Key Formulas

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$Long straight wire (distance $r$)
$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$Centre of a circular loop
$B=\mu_0 n I$Inside a long solenoid ($n$ = turns/m)
$\oint\vec B\cdot d\vec\ell=\mu_0 I_\text{enc}$Ampère's law

$\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm{T\cdot m/A}$  ·  n = turns per metre  ·  r, R in metres.

Worked example Field of a straight wire and a solenoid

(i) Find $B$ at $2.0\,\mathrm{cm}$ from a long wire carrying $10\,\mathrm A$. (ii) Find $B$ inside a solenoid with $500$ turns over $0.25\,\mathrm m$ carrying $3.0\,\mathrm A$.

(i) Straight wire

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}=\dfrac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(10)}{2\pi(0.020)}=1.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm T$.

(ii) Solenoid

$n=\dfrac{500}{0.25}=2000\,\mathrm{turns/m}$, so $B=\mu_0 n I=(4\pi\times10^{-7})(2000)(3.0)=7.5\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$.

Answer$B_\text{wire}=1.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm T$; $B_\text{solenoid}\approx7.5\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm T$

Practice

Practice 1

What current in a long straight wire gives a field of $5.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$ at a distance of $4.0\,\mathrm{cm}$?

Solve $B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$ for $I$: $I=\dfrac{2\pi r B}{\mu_0}=\dfrac{2\pi(0.040)(5.0\times10^{-5})}{4\pi\times10^{-7}}=10\,\mathrm A$.

Answer$I=10\,\mathrm A$

Practice 2

At the centre of a circular loop of radius $0.10\,\mathrm m$ carrying $5.0\,\mathrm A$, what is the magnetic field?

$B=\dfrac{\mu_0 I}{2R}=\dfrac{(4\pi\times10^{-7})(5.0)}{2(0.10)}=3.1\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$.

Answer$B\approx3.1\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm T$
