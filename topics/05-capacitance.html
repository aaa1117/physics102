Unit 1 · Electrostatics — Topic 5

Capacitance & Dielectrics

A capacitor stores charge and energy in an electric field. Mastering series/parallel combinations is the single highest-yield skill for this part of the exam.

Concept

A capacitor is two conductors holding equal and opposite charge $\pm Q$. The charge it holds is proportional to the voltage across it: $Q=C\,\Delta V$. The constant $C$ is the capacitance (farads, F), set by geometry alone.

Series vs parallel — get this right

  • Parallel: same voltage across each; capacitances add: $C_\text{eq}=\sum C_i$.
  • Series: same charge on each; reciprocals add: $\dfrac1{C_\text{eq}}=\sum\dfrac1{C_i}$.

This is the opposite of resistors — a classic trap.

In a series chain the charge $Q$ is the same through every capacitor, and the voltages divide. In a parallel bank the voltage is shared and the charges divide. Energy is stored in the field and can be written three equivalent ways.

Key Formulas

$C=\dfrac{Q}{\Delta V}$Definition of capacitance
$C_\text{parallel}=\textstyle\sum C_i\qquad \dfrac1{C_\text{series}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac1{C_i}$Combinations
$U=\tfrac12 C(\Delta V)^2=\dfrac{Q^2}{2C}=\tfrac12 Q\,\Delta V$Energy stored
$C=\kappa C_0$A dielectric of constant $\kappa$ multiplies the capacitance

C in farads (F); $1\,\mu\mathrm F=10^{-6}\,\mathrm F$  ·  Q in coulombs  ·  ΔV in volts  ·  U in joules.

Midterm Q4 A three-capacitor network

$C_1=8.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ sits directly across the source. $C_2=15\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $C_3=30\,\mu\mathrm F$ are in series with each other, and that series pair is in parallel with $C_1$. The source is $V_0=12\,\mathrm V$.

(a) Equivalent capacitance

Series of $C_2,C_3$: $C_{23}=\dfrac{C_2 C_3}{C_2+C_3}=\dfrac{15\cdot30}{45}=10\,\mu\mathrm F$.

In parallel with $C_1$: $C_\text{eq}=C_1+C_{23}=8.0+10=18\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Answer (a)$C_\text{eq}=18\ \mu\mathrm F$
(b) Charge on $C_1$

$C_1$ has the full $V_0$ across it: $Q_1=C_1 V_0=(8.0)(12)=96\,\mu\mathrm C$.

Answer (b)$Q_1=96\ \mu\mathrm C$
(c) Voltage across $C_2$

The series branch carries $Q_{23}=C_{23}V_0=(10)(12)=120\,\mu\mathrm C$, the same charge on both $C_2$ and $C_3$.

$V_2=\dfrac{Q_{23}}{C_2}=\dfrac{120}{15}=8.0\,\mathrm V$.

Answer (c)$V_2=8.0\ \mathrm V$
(d) Energy stored in $C_3$

$V_3=\dfrac{Q_{23}}{C_3}=\dfrac{120}{30}=4.0\,\mathrm V$, so $U_3=\tfrac12 C_3 V_3^2=\tfrac12(30\times10^{-6})(4.0)^2=2.4\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm J$.

Answer (d)$U_3=2.4\times10^{-4}\ \mathrm J$

Booklet B ($V_0=24\,\mathrm V$)

(a) $C_\text{eq}=18\,\mu\mathrm F$   (b) $Q_1=192\,\mu\mathrm C$   (c) $V_2=16\,\mathrm V$   (d) $U_3=9.6\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm J$.

Practice

Practice 1

A parallel-plate capacitor has plates of area $0.020\,\mathrm m^2$ separated by $1.0\,\mathrm{mm}$ (vacuum). Find its capacitance.

$C=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}=\dfrac{(8.85\times10^{-12})(0.020)}{1.0\times10^{-3}}$.

Answer$C\approx1.8\times10^{-10}\,\mathrm F$ (177 pF).

Why: $C=\varepsilon_0 A/d$ for a vacuum gap.

Practice 2

A $4.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor is charged to $12\,\mathrm V$. Find the stored charge.

$Q=CV=(4.0\times10^{-6})(12)$.

Answer$Q=48\,\mu\mathrm C$.

Why: $Q=CV$.

Practice 3

Find the energy stored in a $2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor charged to $100\,\mathrm V$.

$U=\tfrac12CV^2=\tfrac12(2.0\times10^{-6})(100)^2$.

Answer$U=0.010\,\mathrm J$.

Why: $U=\tfrac12CV^2$.

Practice 4

Find the equivalent capacitance of $6.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $3.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ in series.

$C_\text{eq}=\dfrac{C_1C_2}{C_1+C_2}=\dfrac{6\times3}{6+3}=\dfrac{18}{9}$.

Answer$C_\text{eq}=2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: series capacitors: reciprocals add (product/sum for two).

Practice 5

Find the equivalent capacitance of $6.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $3.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ in parallel.

$C_\text{eq}=C_1+C_2=6.0+3.0$.

Answer$C_\text{eq}=9.0\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: parallel capacitors simply add (opposite of resistors).

Practice 6

A $2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor is in series with a parallel pair of $3.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $3.0\,\mu\mathrm F$. Find the total capacitance.

Parallel pair: $3.0+3.0=6.0\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Series with $2.0$: $\dfrac{2\times6}{2+6}=\dfrac{12}{8}$.

Answer$C_\text{eq}=1.5\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: collapse the parallel part first, then combine in series.

Practice 7

A capacitor has $C_0=5.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ with vacuum. A dielectric of $\kappa=3.0$ fills the gap. Find the new capacitance.

$C=\kappa C_0=3.0(5.0)$.

Answer$C=15\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: a dielectric multiplies capacitance by $\kappa$.

Practice 8

A capacitor has $C=8.0\,\mu\mathrm F$. If the plate separation is doubled (area unchanged), what is the new capacitance?

$C\propto\dfrac1d$, so doubling $d$ halves $C$: $8.0/2$.

Answer$C=4.0\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: $C=\varepsilon_0A/d$ ⇒ $C$ and $d$ are inversely related.

Practice 9

A $2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ and a $4.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor are in series across $12\,\mathrm V$. Find the charge on each and the voltage across each.

$C_\text{eq}=\dfrac{2\times4}{6}=1.33\,\mu\mathrm F$, so $Q=C_\text{eq}V=1.33\times10^{-6}(12)=16\,\mu\mathrm C$ (same on both).

$V_2=Q/C=16/2=8\,\mathrm V$; $V_4=16/4=4\,\mathrm V$.

Answer$Q=16\,\mu\mathrm C$ each; $8\,\mathrm V$ across the $2\,\mu\mathrm F$, $4\,\mathrm V$ across the $4\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Why: series ⇒ same charge; smaller $C$ takes the larger voltage.

Practice 10

A capacitor is charged, then disconnected, and its plates are pulled to twice the separation. What happens to the stored energy?

Charge $Q$ is fixed; $C$ halves, and $U=\dfrac{Q^2}{2C}$.

Halving $C$ doubles $U$ — the extra energy is the work you did pulling the plates apart.

AnswerThe stored energy doubles.

Why: at fixed $Q$, $U=Q^2/2C$ rises as $C$ falls.
