Unit 1 · Electrostatics — Topic 5

Capacitance & Dielectrics

A capacitor stores charge and energy in an electric field. Mastering series/parallel combinations is the single highest-yield skill for this part of the exam.

Concept

A capacitor is two conductors holding equal and opposite charge $\pm Q$. The charge it holds is proportional to the voltage across it: $Q=C\,\Delta V$. The constant $C$ is the capacitance (farads, F), set by geometry alone.

Series vs parallel — get this right

  • Parallel: same voltage across each; capacitances add: $C_\text{eq}=\sum C_i$.
  • Series: same charge on each; reciprocals add: $\dfrac1{C_\text{eq}}=\sum\dfrac1{C_i}$.

This is the opposite of resistors — a classic trap.

In a series chain the charge $Q$ is the same through every capacitor, and the voltages divide. In a parallel bank the voltage is shared and the charges divide. Energy is stored in the field and can be written three equivalent ways.

Key Formulas

$C=\dfrac{Q}{\Delta V}$Definition of capacitance
$C_\text{parallel}=\textstyle\sum C_i\qquad \dfrac1{C_\text{series}}=\textstyle\sum\dfrac1{C_i}$Combinations
$U=\tfrac12 C(\Delta V)^2=\dfrac{Q^2}{2C}=\tfrac12 Q\,\Delta V$Energy stored
$C=\kappa C_0$A dielectric of constant $\kappa$ multiplies the capacitance

C in farads (F); $1\,\mu\mathrm F=10^{-6}\,\mathrm F$  ·  Q in coulombs  ·  ΔV in volts  ·  U in joules.

Midterm Q4 A three-capacitor network

$C_1=8.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ sits directly across the source. $C_2=15\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $C_3=30\,\mu\mathrm F$ are in series with each other, and that series pair is in parallel with $C_1$. The source is $V_0=12\,\mathrm V$.

(a) Equivalent capacitance

Series of $C_2,C_3$: $C_{23}=\dfrac{C_2 C_3}{C_2+C_3}=\dfrac{15\cdot30}{45}=10\,\mu\mathrm F$.

In parallel with $C_1$: $C_\text{eq}=C_1+C_{23}=8.0+10=18\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Answer (a)$C_\text{eq}=18\ \mu\mathrm F$
(b) Charge on $C_1$

$C_1$ has the full $V_0$ across it: $Q_1=C_1 V_0=(8.0)(12)=96\,\mu\mathrm C$.

Answer (b)$Q_1=96\ \mu\mathrm C$
(c) Voltage across $C_2$

The series branch carries $Q_{23}=C_{23}V_0=(10)(12)=120\,\mu\mathrm C$, the same charge on both $C_2$ and $C_3$.

$V_2=\dfrac{Q_{23}}{C_2}=\dfrac{120}{15}=8.0\,\mathrm V$.

Answer (c)$V_2=8.0\ \mathrm V$
(d) Energy stored in $C_3$

$V_3=\dfrac{Q_{23}}{C_3}=\dfrac{120}{30}=4.0\,\mathrm V$, so $U_3=\tfrac12 C_3 V_3^2=\tfrac12(30\times10^{-6})(4.0)^2=2.4\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm J$.

Answer (d)$U_3=2.4\times10^{-4}\ \mathrm J$

Booklet B ($V_0=24\,\mathrm V$)

(a) $C_\text{eq}=18\,\mu\mathrm F$   (b) $Q_1=192\,\mu\mathrm C$   (c) $V_2=16\,\mathrm V$   (d) $U_3=9.6\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm J$.

Practice

Practice 1

Two capacitors, $6\,\mu\mathrm F$ and $3\,\mu\mathrm F$, are connected (i) in parallel, (ii) in series. Find $C_\text{eq}$ for each.

Parallel: $C_\text{eq}=6+3=9\,\mu\mathrm F$.

Series: $\dfrac1{C_\text{eq}}=\dfrac16+\dfrac13=\dfrac12\Rightarrow C_\text{eq}=2\,\mu\mathrm F$.

AnswerParallel $9\,\mu\mathrm F$; series $2\,\mu\mathrm F$

Practice 2

A $10\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor is charged to $50\,\mathrm V$. How much charge and energy does it store?

$Q=C\,\Delta V=(10\times10^{-6})(50)=5.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm C$.

$U=\tfrac12 C(\Delta V)^2=\tfrac12(10\times10^{-6})(50)^2=1.25\times10^{-2}\,\mathrm J$.

Answer$Q=5.0\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm C$, $U=1.25\times10^{-2}\,\mathrm J$
