Current & Resistance

Now charges move. Current, Ohm's law, and power are simple algebra — but they're guaranteed exam points, including the "how many electrons" question.

Concept

Electric current is the rate at which charge flows past a point: $I=\Delta Q/\Delta t$, in amperes (1 A = 1 C/s). By convention current points the way positive charge would move (opposite to the electrons in a metal).

Resistance $R$ measures how much a component opposes current. For many materials Ohm's law holds: $V=IR$. Resistance grows with length and shrinks with cross-section: $R=\rho L/A$, where $\rho$ is the resistivity of the material.

Power three ways

A resistor converts electrical energy to heat at a rate $P=IV$. Using Ohm's law this becomes $P=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$ — pick whichever form matches your knowns. Energy delivered over a time $t$ is $E=Pt$.

To count charge carriers, remember the total charge that flows is $Q=I\,t$, and each electron carries $|e|=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm C$. So the number of electrons is $N=Q/e$.

Key Formulas

$I=\dfrac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}$Current (A)
$V=IR$Ohm's law
$R=\rho\dfrac{L}{A}$Resistance from geometry
$P=IV=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$Power dissipated
$N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{I\,t}{e}$Number of electrons that pass

I in amperes  ·  R in ohms (Ω)  ·  ρ in Ω·m  ·  P in watts  ·  $e=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm C$.

Midterm Q5 Battery, resistor, and electrons

A $120\,\mathrm V$ battery drives a current of $0.80\,\mathrm A$ through a resistor.

(a) Resistance

$R=\dfrac{V}{I}=\dfrac{120}{0.80}=150\,\Omega$.

Answer (a)$R=150\ \Omega$
(b) Energy in one minute

$E=P\,t=V I\,t=(120)(0.80)(60)=5760\,\mathrm J$.

Answer (b)$E=5760\ \mathrm J\approx5.76\ \mathrm{kJ}$
(c) Electrons in one minute

Charge: $Q=I\,t=(0.80)(60)=48\,\mathrm C$. Then $N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{48}{1.6\times10^{-19}}=3.0\times10^{20}$.

Answer (c)$N=3.0\times10^{20}$ electrons

Booklet B ($240\,\mathrm V$)

(a) $R=300\,\Omega$   (b) $E=(240)(0.80)(60)=11520\,\mathrm J$   (c) $N=3.0\times10^{20}$ (current & time unchanged).

Practice

Practice 1

A $12\,\mathrm V$ battery pushes $2.0\,\mathrm A$ through a bulb. Find the resistance and the power dissipated.

$R=\dfrac{V}{I}=\dfrac{12}{2.0}=6.0\,\Omega$.   $P=IV=(2.0)(12)=24\,\mathrm W$.

Answer$R=6.0\,\Omega$, $P=24\,\mathrm W$

Practice 2

How many electrons flow through a wire carrying $3.0\,\mathrm A$ in $10\,\mathrm s$?

$Q=I\,t=(3.0)(10)=30\,\mathrm C$.   $N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{30}{1.6\times10^{-19}}=1.875\times10^{20}$.

Answer$N\approx1.9\times10^{20}$ electrons
