Current & Resistance

Now charges move. Current, Ohm's law, and power are simple algebra — but they're guaranteed exam points, including the "how many electrons" question.

Concept

Electric current is the rate at which charge flows past a point: $I=\Delta Q/\Delta t$, in amperes (1 A = 1 C/s). By convention current points the way positive charge would move (opposite to the electrons in a metal).

Resistance $R$ measures how much a component opposes current. For many materials Ohm's law holds: $V=IR$. Resistance grows with length and shrinks with cross-section: $R=\rho L/A$, where $\rho$ is the resistivity of the material.

Power three ways

A resistor converts electrical energy to heat at a rate $P=IV$. Using Ohm's law this becomes $P=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$ — pick whichever form matches your knowns. Energy delivered over a time $t$ is $E=Pt$.

To count charge carriers, remember the total charge that flows is $Q=I\,t$, and each electron carries $|e|=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm C$. So the number of electrons is $N=Q/e$.

Key Formulas

$I=\dfrac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}$Current (A)
$V=IR$Ohm's law
$R=\rho\dfrac{L}{A}$Resistance from geometry
$P=IV=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$Power dissipated
$N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{I\,t}{e}$Number of electrons that pass

I in amperes  ·  R in ohms (Ω)  ·  ρ in Ω·m  ·  P in watts  ·  $e=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm C$.

Midterm Q5 Battery, resistor, and electrons

A $120\,\mathrm V$ battery drives a current of $0.80\,\mathrm A$ through a resistor.

(a) Resistance

$R=\dfrac{V}{I}=\dfrac{120}{0.80}=150\,\Omega$.

Answer (a)$R=150\ \Omega$
(b) Energy in one minute

$E=P\,t=V I\,t=(120)(0.80)(60)=5760\,\mathrm J$.

Answer (b)$E=5760\ \mathrm J\approx5.76\ \mathrm{kJ}$
(c) Electrons in one minute

Charge: $Q=I\,t=(0.80)(60)=48\,\mathrm C$. Then $N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{48}{1.6\times10^{-19}}=3.0\times10^{20}$.

Answer (c)$N=3.0\times10^{20}$ electrons

Booklet B ($240\,\mathrm V$)

(a) $R=300\,\Omega$   (b) $E=(240)(0.80)(60)=11520\,\mathrm J$   (c) $N=3.0\times10^{20}$ (current & time unchanged).

Practice

Practice 1

A wire carries a steady current of $2.0\,\mathrm A$. How many electrons pass a cross-section each second?

$n=\dfrac{I}{e}=\dfrac{2.0}{1.6\times10^{-19}}$.

Answer$n\approx1.25\times10^{19}$ electrons per second.

Why: $I=$ charge/second, so divide by $e$.

Practice 2

A current of $10\,\mathrm A$ flows in a wire of cross-section $2.0\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm m^2$. Find the current density.

$J=\dfrac{I}{A}=\dfrac{10}{2.0\times10^{-6}}$.

Answer$J=5.0\times10^6\,\mathrm{A/m^2}$.

Why: $J=I/A$.

Practice 3

The current in a wire is held constant while its cross-sectional area is doubled. What happens to the electron drift velocity?

$I=nqAv_\text{d}$ with $I,n,q$ fixed ⇒ $Av_\text{d}=$ const.

Doubling $A$ halves $v_\text{d}$.

AnswerThe drift velocity halves.

Why: area and drift speed are inversely related at fixed current.

Practice 4

A copper wire ($\rho=1.7\times10^{-8}\,\Omega\!\cdot\!\mathrm m$) is $100\,\mathrm m$ long with cross-section $2.0\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm m^2$. Find its resistance.

$R=\rho\dfrac{L}{A}=\dfrac{(1.7\times10^{-8})(100)}{2.0\times10^{-6}}$.

Answer$R\approx0.85\,\Omega$.

Why: $R=\rho L/A$.

Practice 5

A potential difference of $24\,\mathrm V$ drives current through a $6.0\,\Omega$ resistor. Find the current.

$I=\dfrac{V}{R}=\dfrac{24}{6.0}$.

Answer$I=4.0\,\mathrm A$.

Why: Ohm's law $V=IR$.

Practice 6

On a $V$–$I$ graph an ohmic wire passes through $(1\,\mathrm A,4\,\mathrm V)$ and $(3\,\mathrm A,12\,\mathrm V)$. Find its resistance.

$R=\dfrac{\Delta V}{\Delta I}=\dfrac{12-4}{3-1}=\dfrac{8}{2}$.

Answer$R=4.0\,\Omega$.

Why: resistance is the slope of the $V$–$I$ line.

Practice 7

A resistor carries $2.0\,\mathrm A$ and has resistance $10\,\Omega$. Find the power it dissipates.

$P=I^2R=(2.0)^2(10)$.

Answer$P=40\,\mathrm W$.

Why: $P=I^2R$ (also $=VI=V^2/R$).

Practice 8

A wire of resistance $10\,\Omega$ is $5.0\,\mathrm m$ long with cross-section $1.0\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm m^2$. Find the resistivity.

$\rho=\dfrac{RA}{L}=\dfrac{(10)(1.0\times10^{-7})}{5.0}$.

Answer$\rho=2.0\times10^{-7}\,\Omega\!\cdot\!\mathrm m$.

Why: invert $R=\rho L/A$.

Practice 9

A wire has resistance $3.0\,\Omega$. If its length is doubled and its cross-sectional area is halved, find the new resistance.

$R\propto\dfrac{L}{A}$, so the factor is $\dfrac{2}{1/2}=4$.

$R_\text{new}=4(3.0)$.

Answer$R_\text{new}=12\,\Omega$.

Why: stretching a wire (longer + thinner) raises $R$ fast.

Practice 10

A $100\,\mathrm W$ bulb runs for $2.0$ hours. How much energy does it use (in joules)?

$E=Pt=(100)(2.0\times3600)$.

Answer$E=7.2\times10^5\,\mathrm J$ (= $0.20\,\mathrm{kWh}$).

Why: energy $=$ power $\times$ time.
