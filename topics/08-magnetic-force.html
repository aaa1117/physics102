Magnetic Field & Force

Moving charges feel a sideways force in a magnetic field. The cross product and the right-hand rule are everything here.

Concept

A magnetic field $\vec B$ (tesla, T) exerts a force on a moving charge — but only on the part of the velocity perpendicular to $\vec B$. The force is

$\vec F=q\,\vec v\times\vec B,\qquad F=qvB\sin\theta.$

The force is always perpendicular to both $\vec v$ and $\vec B$. Because it's perpendicular to the motion, it does no work and never changes the speed — only the direction.

Right-hand rule

Point your fingers along $\vec v$, curl them toward $\vec B$; your thumb gives $\vec v\times\vec B$. For a positive charge that's the force direction; for a negative charge, reverse it.

A charge moving perpendicular to a uniform $\vec B$ goes in a circle: the magnetic force supplies the centripetal force, $qvB=\dfrac{mv^2}{r}$, giving radius $r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}$.

A current is just moving charge, so a wire of length $L$ carrying current $I$ feels $\vec F=I\vec L\times\vec B$. A current loop in a field feels a torque that tries to align it — the principle of the electric motor.

Key Formulas

$F=qvB\sin\theta$Force on a moving charge
$r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}$Radius of circular motion
$F=BIL\sin\theta$Force on a current-carrying wire
$\tau=NIAB\sin\theta$Torque on an $N$-turn loop of area $A$

B in tesla (T)  ·  θ = angle between $\vec v$ (or wire) and $\vec B$  ·  A = loop area (m²).

Watch out

If $\vec v\parallel\vec B$ ($\theta=0$) the force is zero. Maximum force is at $\theta=90^\circ$. And don't forget to flip the direction for negative charges.

Worked example Proton in a magnetic field

A proton ($q=1.6\times10^{-19}\,\mathrm C$, $m=1.67\times10^{-27}\,\mathrm{kg}$) moves at $v=2.0\times10^{6}\,\mathrm{m/s}$ perpendicular to a $0.50\,\mathrm T$ field. Find the force and the radius of its circular path.

Step 1 — force

$F=qvB\sin90^\circ=(1.6\times10^{-19})(2.0\times10^{6})(0.50)=1.6\times10^{-13}\,\mathrm N$.

Step 2 — radius

$r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}=\dfrac{(1.67\times10^{-27})(2.0\times10^{6})}{(1.6\times10^{-19})(0.50)}=4.2\times10^{-2}\,\mathrm m$.

Answer$F=1.6\times10^{-13}\,\mathrm N$, $r\approx4.2\,\mathrm{cm}$

Practice

Practice 1

A proton moves at $2.0\times10^6\,\mathrm{m/s}$ perpendicular to a $0.50\,\mathrm T$ field. Find the magnetic force on it.

$F=qvB\sin90^\circ=(1.6\times10^{-19})(2.0\times10^6)(0.50)$.

Answer$F=1.6\times10^{-13}\,\mathrm N$.

Why: $F=qvB\sin\theta$, here $\sin\theta=1$.

Practice 2

A positive charge moves in the $+x$ direction in a field pointing in $+y$. What is the direction of the magnetic force?

$\vec F=q\vec v\times\vec B$; $\hat x\times\hat y=\hat z$.

Answer$+z$ direction.

Why: right-hand rule: $\hat x\times\hat y=\hat z$ (charge is positive).

Practice 3

An electron moves at $1.0\times10^7\,\mathrm{m/s}$ perpendicular to a $0.10\,\mathrm T$ field. Find its orbital radius.

$r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}=\dfrac{(9.11\times10^{-31})(1.0\times10^7)}{(1.6\times10^{-19})(0.10)}$.

Answer$r\approx5.7\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm m$.

Why: magnetic force = centripetal ⇒ $r=mv/qB$.

Practice 4

Find the period of a proton's circular motion in a $0.50\,\mathrm T$ field.

$T=\dfrac{2\pi m}{qB}=\dfrac{2\pi(1.67\times10^{-27})}{(1.6\times10^{-19})(0.50)}$.

Answer$T\approx1.3\times10^{-7}\,\mathrm s$.

Why: cyclotron period $T=2\pi m/qB$ — independent of speed.

Practice 5

In a velocity selector $E=3.0\times10^4\,\mathrm{V/m}$ and $B=0.10\,\mathrm T$. What speed passes undeflected?

$qE=qvB\Rightarrow v=\dfrac{E}{B}=\dfrac{3.0\times10^4}{0.10}$.

Answer$v=3.0\times10^5\,\mathrm{m/s}$.

Why: undeflected ⇒ $v=E/B$.

Practice 6

A $0.20\,\mathrm m$ wire carries $5.0\,\mathrm A$ perpendicular to a $0.40\,\mathrm T$ field. Find the force on it.

$F=BIL\sin90^\circ=(0.40)(5.0)(0.20)$.

Answer$F=0.40\,\mathrm N$.

Why: $F=BIL\sin\theta$ for a current-carrying wire.

Practice 7

A wire carries current in the $+x$ direction in a field pointing out of the page ($+z$). What is the direction of the force on the wire?

$\vec F=I\vec L\times\vec B$; $\hat x\times\hat z=-\hat y$.

Answer$-y$ direction.

Why: right-hand rule on $\vec L\times\vec B$.

Practice 8

A $10$-turn coil of area $0.010\,\mathrm m^2$ carries $2.0\,\mathrm A$ in a $0.50\,\mathrm T$ field, with its plane parallel to $\vec B$. Find the torque.

$\tau=NIAB\sin\theta$ with $\theta=90^\circ$: $\tau=(10)(2.0)(0.010)(0.50)$.

Answer$\tau=0.10\,\mathrm{N\!\cdot\! m}$.

Why: $\tau=NIAB\sin\theta$; max when the plane is parallel to $\vec B$.

Practice 9

A proton circles in a uniform magnetic field. How much work does the magnetic force do per revolution, and does the speed change?

The magnetic force is always perpendicular to $\vec v$, so it does no work.

With no work done, the kinetic energy — and the speed — stay constant.

AnswerZero work; the speed is unchanged.

Why: $\vec F\perp\vec v$ ⇒ $W=0$, so magnetic fields only steer, never speed up.

Practice 10

A proton and an electron enter the same field at the same speed, perpendicular to $\vec B$. Which travels on the larger circle?

$r=\dfrac{mv}{qB}\propto m$ (same $|q|$, $v$, $B$).

The proton is far heavier.

AnswerThe proton, by the mass ratio $m_p/m_e\approx1836$.

Why: radius scales with mass at fixed speed and field.
