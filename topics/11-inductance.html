Unit 4 · Induction — Topic 11

Inductance

The last topic. An inductor opposes changes in current and stores energy in its magnetic field — the magnetic counterpart of a capacitor.

Concept

When the current in a coil changes, its own flux changes, inducing an EMF that fights the change (Lenz again). This self-inductance $L$ (henries, H) is defined by

$\varepsilon=-L\dfrac{dI}{dt}.$

A large $L$ means the coil strongly resists any change in current — current in an inductor can't jump instantly.

Energy in the magnetic field

Charging an inductor stores energy $U=\tfrac12 LI^2$ in its magnetic field — exactly parallel to a capacitor's $U=\tfrac12 CV^2$. Inductor ↔ capacitor, current ↔ charge, $L$ ↔ $C$.

An RL circuit has a time constant $\tau=L/R$: current rises (or falls) exponentially over that timescale, just like charge in an RC circuit.

An LC circuit trades energy back and forth between the capacitor's electric field and the inductor's magnetic field, oscillating at angular frequency $\omega=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}$ — an electrical version of a mass on a spring.

Key Formulas

$\varepsilon=-L\dfrac{dI}{dt}$Self-induced EMF (defines $L$)
$U=\tfrac12 LI^2$Energy stored in an inductor
$\tau=\dfrac{L}{R}$RL time constant
$\omega=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}},\qquad f=\dfrac{\omega}{2\pi}$LC oscillation frequency

L in henries (H)  ·  τ in seconds  ·  ω in rad/s.

Worked example Inductor energy, RL time, LC frequency

An inductor has $L=0.50\,\mathrm H$. (i) Find the energy stored when it carries $2.0\,\mathrm A$. (ii) Find the RL time constant with $R=100\,\Omega$. (iii) Find the oscillation frequency if connected to a $C=2.0\,\mu\mathrm F$ capacitor.

(i) Energy

$U=\tfrac12 LI^2=\tfrac12(0.50)(2.0)^2=1.0\,\mathrm J$.

(ii) RL time constant

$\tau=\dfrac{L}{R}=\dfrac{0.50}{100}=5.0\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm s$.

(iii) LC frequency

$\omega=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{(0.50)(2.0\times10^{-6})}}=1.0\times10^{3}\,\mathrm{rad/s}$, so $f=\dfrac{\omega}{2\pi}\approx159\,\mathrm{Hz}$.

Answer$U=1.0\,\mathrm J$, $\tau=5.0\,\mathrm{ms}$, $f\approx159\,\mathrm{Hz}$

Practice

Practice 1

The current in a $0.20\,\mathrm H$ inductor changes at $50\,\mathrm{A/s}$. What is the magnitude of the self-induced EMF?

$|\varepsilon|=L\left|\dfrac{dI}{dt}\right|=(0.20)(50)=10\,\mathrm V$.

Answer$|\varepsilon|=10\,\mathrm V$

Practice 2

How much energy is stored in a $2.0\,\mathrm H$ inductor carrying $3.0\,\mathrm A$? Compare conceptually with a charged capacitor.

$U=\tfrac12 LI^2=\tfrac12(2.0)(3.0)^2=9.0\,\mathrm J$. Like a capacitor stores energy in its electric field ($\tfrac12 CV^2$), an inductor stores it in its magnetic field — released when the current is interrupted.

Answer$U=9.0\,\mathrm J$
